# 📊 Kenya Public Debt & Economic Sustainability Analysis

## 📌 Overview
This project analyzes Kenya’s public debt trends, economic growth, and interest burden over time using Power BI. The goal is to assess whether debt growth is sustainable relative to economic performance.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Track the growth of public debt over time  
- Compare debt growth with GDP growth  
- Analyze debt sustainability using Debt-to-GDP ratio  
- Examine interest payment trends and composition  
- Identify risks in domestic vs external borrowing  

---

## 📂 Data Sources
- Public Debt Data (Domestic & External)  
- Annual GDP Data  
- Interest Payment Data  
- Date Table for time-based analysis  

---

## 📈 Key Metrics
- Total Debt  
- Debt-to-GDP Ratio  
- GDP Growth Rate  
- Debt Growth Rate  
- Growth Gap (Debt Growth – GDP Growth)  
- Interest Paid (Domestic & External)

## 🔍 Key Insights
- Public debt has grown significantly over time  
- Debt growth frequently exceeds GDP growth  
- Debt-to-GDP ratio has increased, indicating rising fiscal pressure  
- Interest payments are increasing, reducing fiscal space  
- Domestic debt constitutes the majority of interest payments  

---

## ⚠️ Conclusion
Kenya’s rising debt levels, combined with slower economic growth relative to borrowing, suggest increasing pressure on fiscal sustainability. The growing interest burden—especially from domestic debt—may limit future economic flexibility and investment capacity.
